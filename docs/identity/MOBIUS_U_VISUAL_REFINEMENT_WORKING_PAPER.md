# Möbi𝒰s Visual Refinement Working Paper

Status: live optical-development contract. The topology, lineage, and motion rig
are executable; this paper owns the remaining craft pass and deliberately
leaves room for Edward's Pencil markup.

## Current blunt verdict

The identity is structurally right and no longer generic. Its strongest
achievement is that the sub-brands are generated from the parent word rather
than displayed beside it. The remaining risk is not conceptual weakness. It is
that one or two letterforms can still look inserted instead of inevitable.

The current pass addresses that risk in two ways:

- `s` is again one continuous conventional double curve with open counters;
- `𝒰` projects from the conserved aperture and `s` unfurls from the operator's
  left-contact terminal pivot, making `us` one generated event in motion.

## Non-negotiable invariants

1. The eye-bearing Möbius body is literal product anatomy.
2. The connected body remains a Möbius band in the operative `UProjection`.
3. Static `Möbi𝒰s` is readable immediately.
4. `𝒰` stays recognizably mathematical and optically smaller than nominal
   lowercase mass.
5. `s` remains readable as a normal `s`, with two open counters and a real
   center reversal.
6. `𝒰s` reads as a relational whole without becoming one illegible ligature.
7. Every offset follows the common phase angle or a documented damped return.
8. The phase ground never becomes decorative underlining.
9. No static effect depends on blur, glow, or a fashionable gradient.

## The word as one composite

```text
M        ö        b        i        𝒰        s
anchor   presence  bridge   approach crossover return
```

The strong horizontal spine is conserved, but glyphs do not obey one sterile
baseline. Their local deviations enact a single trajectory:

```text
equilibrium → fold → lift → directed bias → inversion → damped settlement
```

The word should feel like an engineered system with local freedom, not a
ransom-note collection of clever characters.

## `𝒰` integration

The `𝒰` is allowed to change register because closure transforms
representation. It is not allowed to feel pasted in.

### Static requirements

- white core remains the unique maximum-contrast zone;
- electric-blue keyline is crisp and thin, never fuzzy bloom;
- entry loop retains mathematical recognition;
- stroke contrast is reduced toward the display alphabet;
- right terminal is clipped/engineered enough to converse with `s`;
- height remains optically below the adjacent lowercase mass;
- the lower return and the left edge of `s` create one controlled tangent;
- interior aperture stays open on dark and light backgrounds.

### Motion requirements

`𝒰` must not arrive from the bottom like an imported asset. It begins near the
conserved `ö` aperture, projects outward while the body seats in the word, and
establishes the relational hinge before `s` appears.

The connected `UProjection` later proves that the operative body itself remains
Möbius. The flat wordmark glyph is a readable trace/projection of that state,
not a replacement topology.

## `𝒰s` as complementary hemispheres

The relationship is generated, not fused:

- `𝒰` supplies an open receiving bowl and outward return;
- `s` begins at the outgoing contact edge and develops two alternating curves;
- their colors occupy opposite sides of the white/blue-to-cyan transition;
- their distinct counters remain visible;
- the cyan ground continues beneath `s`, giving the resolved side weight and
  surface without trapping it in a box.

The endpoint should read both `𝒰` and `s`, but the motion should make it
impossible to regard them as unrelated neighbors.

## Counter and negative-space law

The “snowstorm” failure came from broad white faces, excessive light energy,
and counters that lost enough distinction to flatten the word. The correction
is structural:

- `M` has a clean open internal V;
- `ö` is the largest conserved aperture and remains unfilled;
- `b` has one enlarged counter and a fully seated base;
- `i` retains clear stem/dot separation;
- `𝒰` retains its entry and bowl apertures;
- `s` uses one continuous double curve with open upper/lower counters;
- lighting remains below the palette-preserving energy ceiling.

No shader may be used to fake a counter that the mesh has lost.

## Ground and surface

Two colored ground segments live beneath the relational triad:

- indigo segment: approach / perspective / `i`;
- cyan segment: return / relation / `us`.

Their break is the inversion seam. Their phase tilt is `φ`. Together with the
operator's upright they establish bottom and side around the circular aperture,
supporting the authored “squaring the circle” reading without drawing a square.

Pencil refinements should test:

- whether both segments need small curvature rather than perfect bars;
- whether the break belongs directly below the operator center or slightly to
  the return side;
- whether the cyan segment should terminate under the `s` or carry a slightly
  longer relational overhang;
- whether the bars share one optical thickness at every responsive size.

## Letter-specific Pencil pass

When Edward's markup arrives, ingest it as constraints rather than tracing the
entire raster image.

### `M`

- test subtle external corner softening;
- preserve the open V and broad low-slung stance;
- do not round it into a friendly app-font `M`;
- retain the right-inner diagonal because it is the source of Delta edge A.

### `ö`

- keep the collapsed connected body in the canonical static render;
- keep two literal eyes and their animation hierarchy;
- preserve enough separation that the eyes read as presence, not an umlaut
  accidentally detached from the word.

### `b`

- keep the bottom filled/seated;
- keep the enlarged counter fully open;
- preserve exact lineage for the B-to-D reflection.

### `i`

- keep the same display family as `Möb`;
- preserve the `φ` approach bias and elevated dot;
- never turn the dot into a second literal animated eye.

### `𝒰`

- compare Pencil terminal curve against the current keyed outline;
- preserve the mathematical entry loop even if the external body becomes more
  geometric;
- prefer a better contour over another scale reduction;
- keep the white core and blue edge as semantic, not decorative, contrast.

### `s`

- retain the restored conventional double curve;
- use the Pencil pass to tune the waist and terminal relation to `𝒰`;
- do not return to disconnected upper/lower lobes merely because triangulation
  is easier;
- verify the counter at SVG, Blender, and 24-pixel raster scales.

## Color law

| Role | Dark surface | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| substrate | cobalt/deep indigo | parent field and stable frame |
| `i` | brighter phase indigo | directed intent/perspective |
| `𝒰` edge/core | electric blue / white | change, compression, guarded closure |
| `s` | cyan | relational consequence |

The embodied ribbon may interpolate front/back color to expose orientation.
The flat wordmark uses inks, not atmospheric bloom. A four-color
`一 → 意 → 易 → 義` temperature chain belongs in motion, not sprayed across the
static alphabet.

## Korean refinement

The current committed state uses the connected body as `ㅇ`, the phase-corrected
ground as `ㅡ`, and the separately derived i-stem as `ㅣ`. The vertical has been
moved inward so the three components read as one compact block.

Refinement tests:

1. compare 200, 320, and 450 ms holds;
2. test Korean-reader recognition without prior explanation;
3. preserve the actual component order after restoration;
4. reject any change that turns the wordmark itself into faux-Hangul;
5. keep the structural state available as a still for reduced motion.

## Reverse-sheet storyboard (working, not yet in master)

1. Hold the one-turn `Möbi𝒰s` state.
2. Lift the word as one sheet; do not reverse glyphs independently.
3. Rotate through the fold plane so the back face reads `s𝒰iböM`.
4. Isolate the opening `SUI` without deleting the remaining letters.
5. Annotate *suī* only in the explanatory cut, never inside the logo.
6. Optionally lift the two literal eyes from terminal `ö`, leaving `oM` to
   normalize into `OM` for one respectful beat.
7. Restore the eyes and complete the second turn back to the parent mark.

The `SUI` reveal is structurally strong. The `OM` reveal remains experimental
because it needs both a designed diacritic transition and cultural restraint.

## Break tests

| Test | Predicted failure | Required response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `𝒰` at 16 px | keyline collapses | use solid small-size sigil |
| `s` Blender triangulation | waist closes or lobes flatten | fail build; fix geometry, not light |
| two ground segments on white | look like broken typo underline | light-surface variant may omit ground |
| `의` at 200 ms | recognition too brief | lengthen hold, do not enlarge into annotation |
| reverse `SUI` | reads like unrelated acronym | keep it in a sheet-turn explanatory cut |
| `OM` | feels appropriative or contrived | remove from shipped identity while retaining design note |
| eyes plus i-dot | face collage | correct scale/animation hierarchy, not delete the eyes |

## Acceptance target

The next accepted wordmark should satisfy all of these simultaneously:

- recognizable in under one second;
- drawable from memory;
- works in monochrome before material treatment;
- preserves every required counter;
- `𝒰` reads special but inevitable;
- `s` reads conventional but generated;
- `𝒰s` feels like one relational field;
- no explanatory symbol is required for basic recognition;
- every deeper transition can be derived from a named source object in the rig.
